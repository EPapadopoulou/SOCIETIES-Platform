Preferences & Intent:

Preferences are common actions you tend to take with the system and 3rd party services, under particular circumstances known to the context database. The preferences section lets you look at preferences that have been automatically identified for you already -- for example, that you tend to watch a certain channel when you turn on the MyTV channel between 15:00 and 17:00. These can be removed if you do not like them, or edited to e.g. change the conditions when the preference will be enacted.

Intent is similar to preferences, but an intent describes a sequence of actions, possibly with circumstances surrounding them as above. When you start acting out the first one or two actions in an intent sequence, the intent lets the system predict follow-up actions and execute them automatically -- for example, you always start the Collaborative Quiz service when you stand in front of a Learning Zone screen, then select 'take quiz', then select the 'computing' set of questions, etc. As with preferences, these can be edited, deleted, and created in a similar manner.

Privacy:

Privacy policies are listed here. These specify what pieces of information stored in your container can be shown to what people, and to what extent. You can directly edit what the privacy policy is for each piece of context data about you, whether you manually entered that data or it was detected on you -- for example, you can set your name as visible to all users, but not let anyone see your GPS co-ordinates, or restrict who can see them.

Android App use:

The Android App should be fairly straightforward to use if you are familiar with the Web App -- it's essentially a reduced version of it, letting you manage communities, friends, and 3rd party services. See the FAQ below for some user interfaces issues that have been discovered, however.

FAQ on issues you may have with SOCIETIES

Web App

Ease of use

- If you reach the main page without logging in, remember to click 'Log in' in the upper right corner of the page first. Otherwise any subpage you go to will resend you back to the main page without explanation.

Android App

Ease of use

- The Android App can be slow and clunky when you scroll through large lists, such as lists of communities or friends. This is a performance issue stemming from the nuances of the implementation, and we ask for your patience in working with this.

3rd Party Services

AskFree

Ease of use

Known issues

Collaborative Quiz

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

Known issues

- Due to the Kinect's limitations, sometimes gestures or poses you make may take some time to register or you may have to try several times. You may also have to speak words 2 or 3 times for it to be recognised. Remember to speak clearly and not too quietly.

Potential issues

- One person at a time should stand in front of the Kinect; if someone cuts in front or walks nearby the Kinect could confuse them for the current user, or lose track of the current user.

MyTV

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

Known issues

- Only one user can operate the service at once, and that user's ID is not displayed on the current version of the service. So, make sure to keep track of who started the service so only they try to operate on it.

NearMe

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

Known issues

- The service has a wide range for detecting nearby people, and can pick up people that aren't actually in the same space -- for example they may be in another room nearby, or fairly far across the room.

Context-Aware Wall

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

Geofencing

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

Potential issues

- The precision and accuracy of user location read by the Geofencing service may not be perfect. So, users nearby a Geofence but not actually in it may be picked up as within it, and people in the area may be picked out as outside, etc. It may be wise to make geofences a little generous with the range to ensure the people you want detected are detected.

Shared Calendar

Potential issues

- Shared Calendar has not been visible or testable until recently, and so may have issues we have not identified.

Please report any problems you encounter to societies@macs.hw.ac.uk.

CrowdTasking

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

CoBrowser

Ease of use

- The service should be fairly self-explanatory; consult the user guide if you are unsure of anything.

Known issues

- When one user clicks a hyperlink and changes pages, all users will go to that page without warning. You may want to use the chat function to point out when you're moving pages.
